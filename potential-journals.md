# Potential venues for submission

Working shortlist for *“Do Frontier-Safety Evaluations Have the Resolving Power Their
Thresholds Imply?”* Updated 17 July 2026.

## What the paper is

The paper is an empirical and statistical audit of public frontier-AI safety evaluations. It
applies established methods—Wilson and Fieller intervals, power analysis, and
conformity-assessment ideas—to an AI-governance problem. Its contribution is not a new
statistical method or a new alignment technique.

The strongest version of the paper establishes that public reporting often does not provide
enough information to reconstruct or verify a consistent, uncertainty-aware decision rule.
This is more defensible than saying that five evaluations “failed.”

There are three plausible audiences:

1. AI safety, responsible AI, and evaluation researchers.
2. Statistics and risk researchers working on public-policy decisions.
3. AI governance and technology-policy researchers.

No single established journal covers all three communities. The best target therefore depends
on whether the priority is AI-community reach, statistical validation, or policy readership.

### How to preserve eligibility

- List the University of Melbourne as the primary affiliation wherever the publisher requires
  it, and use an `@unimelb.edu.au` or `@student.unimelb.edu.au` email address.
- Identify the author as the corresponding author in the submission system.
- Select the institutionally funded open-access option when prompted; do not pay an APC invoice
  before checking it with the University Library.
- Recheck coverage at acceptance. Publisher agreements are time-limited, and a paper submitted
  in 2026 may not be accepted until a later agreement year.

On present costs, the strongest likely APC-free choices are **ACM Journal on Responsible
Computing**, **Data & Policy**, **Statistics and Public Policy**, **AI and Ethics**, **Risk
Analysis**, and **Science and Public Policy**. HDSR, TMLR, and JAIR are fee-free independently
of Melbourne, although their fit requirements remain as described elsewhere in this document.

## AI-safety-focused options

### Best credible options

#### ACM Journal on Responsible Computing

This is the closest current combination of AI focus, evaluation/accountability scope, credible
publisher, and fit to the manuscript. It welcomes quantitative and interdisciplinary work at
the intersection of computing, policy, ethics, law, and responsible innovation. The paper
would not need to pretend to introduce a new ML algorithm.

**Recommended positioning:**

- Frontier safety evaluation as responsible-computing infrastructure.
- Statistical auditability of safety and deployment claims.
- Transparency and accountability in private safety frameworks.
- Evaluation assurance and minimum reporting standards.

#### AI and Ethics

This journal is more explicitly concerned with frontier-model safety and governance than the
earlier shortlist suggested. Its current scope lists risk governance for frontier models and
general-purpose AI. The complication is its core requirement that every submission make a
substantive ethical argument.

Possible ethical contribution:

- Developers making public safety claims have an epistemic obligation to disclose the
  uncertainty and decision rules needed to assess those claims.
- Private organizations making socially consequential deployment decisions have stronger
  transparency duties than ordinary benchmark publishers.
- False reassurance and excessive precaution distribute risks differently, so a defensible
  reporting standard must make the relevant risk tolerance explicit.

Without this additional normative analysis, the paper is likely to be considered descriptive
or methodological rather than an AI-ethics contribution.

#### TMLR

TMLR is not branded as an AI-safety journal, but it reaches the technical audience most able to
change evaluation practice. Its scope includes methods for assessing performance and
reproducibility studies. Its [acceptance criteria](https://jmlr.org/tmlr/acceptance-criteria.html)
focus on evidential support and audience interest rather than requiring a state-of-the-art
algorithm.

For a credible TMLR submission, the paper should:

- make task-, run-, and benchmark-generalisation estimands the central technical problem;
- present the SWE-bench result as a sampling-unit sensitivity analysis rather than an
  unconditional count;
- derive general recommendations for uncertainty in thresholded model evaluations;
- reduce broad policy rhetoric; and
- ideally add simulations or further evaluation datasets demonstrating when common reporting
  practices produce misleading conclusions.

#### Frontiers in Artificial Intelligence

[Frontiers in Artificial Intelligence](https://www.frontiersin.org/journals/artificial-intelligence)
is an established AI journal, although it has no dedicated safety section. A submission might
fit the Machine Learning and Artificial Intelligence section. The Technology and Law section
accepts evaluation methodologies and critical assessments, but generally expects computational
methods connected to legal or normative practice. APCs apply.

#### Journal of Artificial Intelligence Research

[JAIR](https://www.jair.org/index.php/jair/about) is a respected, diamond-open-access AI
journal, but it is a high-risk stretch. It requires an original and significant advance for the
AI research community. Its [submission guidance](https://www.jair.org/index.php/jair/about/submissions)
warns that applications of known techniques without broader methodological insight are likely
to be rejected without review.

The current paper is not a good JAIR submission. It would need to become a more general theory
and methodology of uncertainty in AI evaluation, supported by substantially more empirical or
theoretical analysis.

### New safety-branded journals: watch, but do not lead with them

Two journals now use very direct AI-safety or alignment branding:

- **[Journal of Human-Compatible AI](https://www.jhcai.org/index.php/journal/about)** — alignment,
  safety, control, and governance.
- **[Journal of AI Safety, Ethics, and Alignment Science](https://journal.luminapress.co.uk/index.php/JASEAS/announcement)** — announced an inaugural call in 2026.

Neither is currently recommended for this manuscript. As of 17 July 2026, the Journal of
Human-Compatible AI [has not published an issue](https://www.jhcai.org/index.php/journal/issue/current),
despite having advertised an inaugural March 2026 issue. The other journal is also still at the
inaugural-call stage. This does not establish that either journal is illegitimate; it means
their review standards, indexing, readership, and archival record are not yet demonstrated.

A first solo-author paper should normally go to a venue with an established review and
publication record unless there is a compelling editor or community reason to help launch a
new journal.

## Archival conference alternatives

AI safety and responsible-AI research remains conference-oriented. If conference publication
and a shorter paper are acceptable, the following may reach the intended community more
directly than some journals.

| Venue | Fit | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **ACM FAccT** | Strong | Excellent audience for an empirical transparency and accountability audit of model developers. Competitive and deadline-driven. |
| **AAAI/ACM AIES** | Strong | Directly relevant to AI governance, evaluation, accountability, and ethics. Archival proceedings. |
| **NeurIPS Datasets & Benchmarks** | Conditional | Possible if the corpus itself is formalized as a reusable dataset and the paper contributes general benchmark-evaluation methodology. Highly competitive. |
| **SaTML** | Weak-to-medium | Trustworthy-ML audience, but more technically focused on security, privacy, robustness, and attacks. |
| **SafeAI @ AAAI / AISafety @ IJCAI workshops** | Useful for feedback | Direct safety-community visibility, but weaker archival weight and typically more technical/alignment-focused than this paper. |

Conference deadlines and tracks change annually and must be checked before planning a
submission. Do not assume the previous year’s format or deadline applies.

## Recommended submission strategies

### If AI-safety readership is the priority

1. ACM Journal on Responsible Computing.
2. AI and Ethics, after adding a substantive ethical argument.
3. Data & Policy.
4. Journal of Responsible Technology.

### If statistical credibility is the priority

1. Statistics and Public Policy.
2. Harvard Data Science Review proposal.
3. Data & Policy.
4. Risk Analysis, after a decision-theoretic expansion.

### If technical ML-evaluation reach is the priority

1. TMLR, after a substantial technical reframing.
2. ACM Journal on Responsible Computing.
3. A FAccT or AIES conference submission.
4. JAIR only after a much larger methodological contribution.

### Overall recommendation

The best current choices are:

- **ACM Journal on Responsible Computing** for the most relevant AI-facing audience.
- **Data & Policy** for the best match to the manuscript’s present empirical-governance
  contribution.
- **Statistics and Public Policy** for the strongest statistical validation, once the major
  methodological issues are resolved.

Avoid choosing a venue solely because its title contains “AI safety.” An established journal
with the right reviewers and readership is more valuable than a perfectly named journal with
no demonstrated publication record.
