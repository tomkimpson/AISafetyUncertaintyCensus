# Journal-submission checklist

This revision is a working-paper branch, not yet submission-ready. The statistical
pipeline is reproducible, but two evidence-quality gates require human or external work.

## Blocking gates

- [ ] Two independent human coders complete all 98 screened census records.
- [ ] The same coders independently reconstruct all ten model-level threshold rows.
- [ ] All disagreements are adjudicated with source-based rationales.
- [ ] `make verify-human` passes.
- [ ] Adjudicated changes are applied to canonical inputs and `make all`, tests,
      checksums, and manuscript-number guards are rerun.
- [ ] Developer/evaluator factual-review requests are sent under
      `RIGHT_OF_REPLY.md`, with at least 14 calendar days to respond.
- [ ] Responses and non-responses are reported neutrally; silence is not treated as
      agreement.
- [ ] All 26 frame documents and every new literature/regulatory citation resolve,
      with archival anchors refreshed where needed.
- [ ] The final manuscript no longer describes validation or right-of-reply as pending.

## Final editorial pass

- [ ] Preserve the narrow contribution claim: empirical cross-framework reporting
      audit plus a reconstructable threshold-decision layer.
- [ ] Preserve the fixed-benchmark/generalisation distinction and the
      Anthropic-only scope of the model-level subset.
- [ ] Keep below demonstrated / indeterminate / above demonstrated separate; do not
      call their union one 95% classification.
- [ ] Keep the uplift analysis labelled as a nonrepresentative case illustration and
      the 2.8× line labelled supplementary and nonbinding.
- [ ] Keep conformity assessment limited to the decision-rule analogy; do not imply
      physical-metrology traceability.
- [ ] Adapt length, abstract, disclosure, data-availability, and bibliography format
      to the selected journal.

## Positioning

The best fit is a journal that welcomes empirical AI-governance, evaluation-methodology,
or responsible-AI measurement work. The paper is not positioned as a new interval-estimation
method or a representative prevalence survey. A cover letter should lead with the
version-pinned corpus, the record/source denominator distinction, the public auditability
funnel, and the practical decision-rule recommendation.
